About

I'm a mechanical engineering student finishing my B.S. and starting a 4+1 M.S. at Binghamton University's Thomas J. Watson College of Engineering. My focus is hands-on fabrication — turning a design on paper into a working, physical part.

During my time at Binghamton, I competed with Formula SAE on the accumulator mechanical subteam, where I worked on the structural and mechanical design of the car's battery enclosure.

I'm currently a makerspace intern at GE Appliances' CoCreate facility in Stamford, CT, where I support fabrication for both internal R&D projects and retail-facing builds — from custom fixtures to full-scale display cases.

Across my coursework and internships I've built proficiency with manual and CNC machining, welding, woodworking, waterjet and laser cutting, embroidery, and CAD/CAM/FEA software including Fusion 360, Creo, SolidWorks, and Ansys Workbench.
